Submitted referrals let partners manually submit prospective customers to your program, even when the referral did not come through a tracked partner link.

Partners see these in their **Customers** section as **Submitted Referrals**, and program owners review them in the workspace under **Customers** → **Partner Referrals**.

## What are submitted referrals?

A submitted referral is a manual referral submitted by a partner through your program's referral form.

Every submitted referral always includes these required fields:

- **Name**
- **Email**
- **Company**

You can also add custom fields to collect more context from partners, but these three fields are always required.

## How they differ from tracked leads

Tracked leads are created automatically when someone clicks a partner link and later completes a conversion event that you send to Dub.

Submitted referrals are different:

- They are entered manually by the partner
- They start as a referral record, not an attributed lead event
- They only become a lead or sale in Dub when you update their status

This makes submitted referrals useful for sales-assisted workflows where partners introduce prospects before a tracked signup or purchase happens.

## Where to find submitted referrals

As a program owner, go to **Customers** in your Partner Program navigation, then open the **Partner Referrals** tab.

This view shows all partner-submitted referrals in one table, including:

- Lead email
- Company
- Partner
- Submitted date
- Current status

Click any row to open the referral details panel and review the submission more closely.

If you want to review converted customers after a referral has been qualified or closed won, see [Customer insights](/help/article/customer-insights).

## Referral statuses

Submitted referrals move through a simple pipeline inside Dub:

| Status | What it means |
| --- | --- |
| **New** | The referral was submitted and has not been reviewed yet. |
| **Qualified** | The referral is valid and worth pursuing. This also creates a lead event in Dub. |
| **Meeting** | The referred prospect has progressed to a meeting stage. |
| **Negotiation** | The referred prospect is in an active deal cycle. |
| **Unqualified** | The referral is not a fit and will not move forward. |
| **Closed won** | The referral became a customer. This also records a sale in Dub. |
| **Closed lost** | The opportunity did not convert. |

### Status rules

- All referrals start in **New**
- **Unqualified**, **Closed won**, and **Closed lost** are final states
- You can move referrals forward or backward between **Qualified**, **Meeting**, and **Negotiation**
- A referral must have an associated customer before it can be moved to **Closed won**

In practice, that means you should typically mark a submitted referral as **Qualified** before marking it as **Closed won**.

## What happens when you change a status

When you update a referral's status, Dub logs the change and can notify the partner by email. You can also include optional notes for the partner with the status update.

Two statuses also create downstream conversion records:

### Qualified

When you move a submitted referral to **Qualified**, Dub:

- creates a lead event with the event name **Qualified Lead**
- uses the source **submitted referral**
- creates or associates a customer record

You can optionally provide an external ID at this step. If you don't, Dub uses the referral email as the customer external ID.

### Closed won

When you move a submitted referral to **Closed won**, Dub:

- records a sale event with the event name **Closed Won**
- uses the source **closed won deal**
- requires a sale amount
- optionally saves a Stripe customer ID to the customer record

## Rewards for submitted referrals

Submitted referrals use your normal partner reward system.

- Marking a referral as **Qualified** can trigger your **lead reward**
- Marking a referral as **Closed won** can trigger your **sale reward**

If you want submitted referrals to be rewarded differently from tracked partner-link conversions, configure separate reward conditions in your [partner rewards](/help/article/partner-rewards) setup based on the customer's **Source**:

- **submitted referral** for qualified submitted leads
- **closed won deal** for submitted referrals that become customers

This lets you keep manually submitted referrals on their own payout logic if needed.

## Required fields

Every submitted referral form requires:

| Field | Required |
| --- | --- |
| Name | Yes |
| Email | Yes |
| Company | Yes |

These fields are locked as required in the app. You can add extra custom fields, but you cannot remove these three base fields.
