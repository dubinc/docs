Partner tags help you organize partners with flexible labels. You can use tags to track partner types, campaigns, outreach status, lifecycle stage, or any other internal classification that helps your team manage the program.

Unlike [partner groups](/help/article/partner-groups), tags do not control rewards, discounts, referral link settings, or application pages. Use groups for program configuration, and use tags for flexible organization and filtering.

## When to use partner tags

Use partner tags when you want to label partners without changing how their program membership works.

Common examples include:

- **Source**: `YouTube`, `Newsletter`, `Agency`, or `Customer referral`
- **Status**: `Needs follow-up`, `Contract sent`, or `High intent`
- **Campaign**: `Q2 launch`, `Black Friday`, or `Webinar cohort`
- **Relationship**: `VIP`, `Strategic`, or `Community`

## Adding tags to partners

From the **Partner Program** menu, open **All Partners** and select the partner you want to update.

In the partner details view, add or remove tags from the partner's profile. Tags are internal to your workspace and help your team keep partner context organized.

## Filtering partners by tags

Partner tags can be used alongside other partner filters such as group, status, and location. This makes it easier to segment your partner table for follow-up, reporting, and operational workflows.

For example, you can filter for partners tagged `VIP` who are also in a specific [partner group](/help/article/partner-groups), or partners tagged `Needs follow-up` after an outreach campaign.

## Partner tags vs. partner groups

| Feature | Partner tags | Partner groups |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Primary use | Flexible internal organization | Program configuration and segmentation |
| Controls rewards | No | Yes |
| Controls discounts | No | Yes |
| Controls referral link settings | No | Yes |
| Best for | Labels, workflows, and filtering | Rewards, discounts, applications, and partner movement |

Learn more about [managing your program partners](/help/article/managing-program-partners) and [partner groups](/help/article/partner-groups).
